# About - Questionity

Once you have signed up in Questionity, you can add more information about yourself when configuring your profile, although providing such information is voluntary and is not necessary to use Questionity.

You can complete your profile if you want other users to know more about you, if so, this is the information that we will collect:

Once you become a registered user in Questionity you will be able to send invitations to your friends so they can also register with Questionity.

For this purpose, you will provide your friend’s email through the invitation system provided by Questionity. In any case, your friend will have to decide whether or not to register with Questionity, accepting the invitation and finishing the registration process.

Questionity will not store the email of your friend to whom you send the invitation, hence you will be responsible for whether your friend wants to receive such invitation or not, so you need to make a responsible use of Questionity invitation process.

The authenticity of the information provided is under your sole responsibility. Questionity is not responsible for any false or inaccurate statements made by you nor the damage they can cause to third parties.

However, when a user name is pejorative, violates the dignity of others or generally fails to meet the [terms of use of Questionity](https://web.archive.org/web/20111217212231id_/http%3A//about.questionity.com/terms-of-use?lang=en), we reserve the right to suspend or cancel the account, agreed under such terms.

##  **Use of the gathered information**

 **Images and Videos**

The images and/or links that you upload to Questionity are stored in our servers. The use of this content is subject to the [terms of use of Questionity](https://web.archive.org/web/20111217212231id_/http%3A//about.questionity.com/terms-of-use?lang=en).

If you do not want us to store metadata associated with images, remove that information before uploading the content that hosts them to Questionity.

 **Statistics**

We can extract statistics from the activity carried out by each user; it is even likely that we would share these statistics with associated companies. However, that information will be provided separately, that is, those third parties would not have access to your personal information, in any case.

##  **Consent of registered user for information treatment in Questionity**

The fact of signing up in Questionity implies that you consent to and agree to the processing of your personal information, according to the use permitted in Questionity, as stated in this privacy policy and [terms of use](https://web.archive.org/web/20111217212231id_/http%3A//about.questionity.com/terms-of-use?lang=en).

The user registration in Questionity implies that you may receive, via e-mail associated to the user account, updates of Questionity news, new services and commercial offers, as well as alerts related to the service.

This does not prejudice your option to revoke  consent of receiving certain communications and alerts, by configuring this feature in the settings menu of Questionity.

You may receive the following types of communications from Questionity:

  * A user has accepted your invitation to join Questionity.
  * You have been reported.



You can also configure your account to receive notifications about the following issues:

  * You are followed by a new user.
  * One of your Q!qrams has been commented.
  * Occasional updates of new features, versions and surveys of Questionity.

##  **Navigation**

Note that navigation through Questionity may involve the use of cookies. Cookies are small amounts of information that are stored in your browser, so that the server remembers easily certain information about user’s navigation. In that way, your navigation preferences can be stored.

However, if you do not want this information to be saved, you can configure your browser for this purpose.

In general, most browsers allow the management of cookies as follows:

1\.  Not accepting cookies.

2\. Asking individually whether to accept or not each cookie.

3\. Accepting cookies always by default.

4\.  Specifying the domains of the cookies to be accepted.

Questionity uses Google Analytics, an analytics web service provided by Google, Inc., a Delaware company with headquarters at 1600 Amphitheatre Parkway, Mountain View (California), CA 94043, Estados Unidos (“Google”). Google Analytics uses "cookies" which are text files placed on your computer, to help the website analyze how users use the web site. The information generated by the cookie about your use of the website (including your IP address) is transmitted to and stored by Google on the United States servers. Google will use this information on our behalf in order to track your use of the website, compiling reports on website activity for website operators and providing other services relating to website activity and Internet use. Google may also transfer this information to third parties where required to do so by law or where such third parties process the information on Google's behalf. Google will not associate your IP address with any other data held by Google. You may refuse treatment of data or information by rejecting the use of cookies and selecting the appropriate settings on your browser. However, you should know that, by doing so, you may not be able to use the full functionality of this website. By using this website, you consent the treatment of your information in the manner and for the purposes described above.
