 **(2) “Cookies” Policy**

 **What are "cookies"?**

 **“** Cookies” are computer data, in particular text files, stored on users’ end devices, which are intended to operate websites. Those files allow website to recognise the user’s device and display website in a manner tailored to individual preferences of the user. “Cookies” usually include the name of the server which left them, the date and time of their storing on the user’s end device as well as a unique reference number.

We use both "session" cookies and "persistent" cookies on the website. Session cookies will be deleted from your computer when you close your browser. Persistent cookies will remain stored on your computer until deleted, or until they reach a specified expiry date.

We will use the session cookies to: keep track of you whilst you navigate the website; keep track of items in your shopping basket; prevent fraud and increase website security. We will use the persistent cookies to: enable our website to recognise you when you visit; keep track of your preferences in relation to your use of our website.

 **Why do we use "cookies"?**

“Cookies” files are used in order to adjust the contents of websites to the preferences of the user as well as to optimise the use of websites. Additionally, they are used in order to create anonymous, aggregated statistics, which help understand how the user uses websites, which subsequently enables to improve their structures and contents with the exclusion of personal identification of the user.

Cookies may be used to:

  1. adjust the contents of websites of the Service to preferences of the user as well as to optimise the use of websites; in particular, those files allow the website to recognise the device of the user of the Service and to display website in a manner tailored to individual needs of the user;
  2. improve the Service through maximal individualisation of contents displayed for a given user of the Service;
  3. create statistics, which help understand how users of the Service use websites, which consequently enables to improve their structures and contents;
  4. maintain the session of the user of the Service (after logging on), so that the user does not have to enter his/her login and password on each sub-site of the Service;
  5. optimise advertising and marketing offers for a given user of the Service, both those of Passion Connection LTD. as well as other advertisers and business partners of Passion Connection LTD.



Basically, there are three types of cookies files: “session cookies”, “persistent cookies” and “external cookies”.

 **“Session cookies”** are temporary files stored on the user’s end device until logging off, leaving the Service or switching off the software (the Internet browser) and they are used to service services requiring authorization within a given Service.

 **“Persistent cookies”** arestoredon the user’s end devise within time specified in cookies parameters or until cookies are removed by the user. Such cookies are intended to store settings and interface personalisation selected by the user of the service.

 **“External cookies” (so called third-party cookies)** enable to collect information on the manner of the use of the Service and provide users with advertisements optimally tailored to interests of individual users.

Cookies are inserted on the end device of the user of the Service and may be used also by advertisers and partners cooperating with the operator of the Service.

We use Google Analytics or we may to use other tools to analyse the use of this website. Google Analytics generates statistical and other information about website use by means of cookies, which are stored on users' computers. The information generated relating to our website is used to create reports about the use of the website. Google will store this information. Google's privacy policy is available at: http://www.google.com/privacypolicy.html.

Our advertisers / payment services providers may also send you cookies.

We may publish Google AdSense interest-based advertisements on our website. These are tailored by Google to reflect your interests. To determine your interests, Google will track your behaviour on our website and on other websites across the web using the DART cookie. You can view, delete or add interest categories associated with your browser using Google's Ads Preference Manager, available at: http://www.google.com/ads/preferences/. You can opt out of the AdSense partner network cookie at: http://www.google.com/privacy/ads/ or by using the NAI's (Network Advertising Initiative's) multi-cookie opt-out mechanism at: http://www.networkadvertising.org/managing/opt_out.asp. However, these opt-out mechanisms use cookies, and if you clear the cookies from your browser your opt-out will not be maintained. To ensure that an opt-out is maintained in respect of a particular browser, you should use the Google browser plug-in available at: http://www.google.com/ads/preferences/plugin.

 **Managing cookies:**

Available Web browsers accept cookies in initial settings. However, each user may reject inserting cookies on his/her end device. In principle, in order to alter the settings one shall use the option of switching off cookies mechanism in a given Web browser, both in case of computers, smartphones as well as other devices. Those settings may be altered in such a manner that they halt automatic operating of cookies in Web browser settings or inform about each inserting of cookies on the device of the user of the Service. Detailed information on the option and ways of cookies operating is available in settings of the software (of web browser). **It is worth mentioning that the removal of cookies may cause the loss of the possibility to use important functionalities of the Service.**

For more information about cookies, visit <http://www.aboutcookies.org/>
