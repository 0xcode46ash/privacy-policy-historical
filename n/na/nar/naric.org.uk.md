# ECCTIS Ltd - Privacy policy

[](https://web.archive.org/web/20121023100923id_/http%3A//www.ecctis.co.uk/default.aspx)

### Cookies

Cookies are alphanumeric identifiers that we transfer to your computer's hard drive through your Web browser. They are used the help us identify you when you come back to our websites. They make sure that your username and password are remembered and are used in our online application system. We make use of cookies to help manage our website, make our website easier to use and support the provision of relevant information and functionality to you to improve your visitor experience, as well as provide us with information about how our website is used so that we can make sure it is as up-to-date, relevant and as error free as we can. 

By using our website you agree to the use of cookies as described in this Cookie Policy.

We recommend that you leave them turned on, but you can turn them off by using the Particular settings within your browser.

If you do leave cookies turned on, be sure to sign off when you finish using a shared computer.

### Does ECCTIS Ltd share the Information it receives?

Information about our customers is an important part of our business and we only share it with carefully selected companies if customers have expressed that they wish for this to happen.

### How Secure Is Information About Me?

We work to protect the security of your information during transmission by using Secure Sockets Layer (SSL) software, which encrypts information you input.

We reveal only the last four digits of your credit card numbers when confirming an order. Of course, we transmit the entire credit card number to the appropriate credit card company during order processing.

We maintain physical, electronic and procedural safeguards in connection with the collection, storage and disclosure of personally identifiable customer information. Our security procedures mean that we may occasionally request proof of identity before we disclose personal information to you.

It is important for you to protect against unauthorised access to your password and to your computer. Be sure to sign off when you finish using a shared computer.
