# Privacy - www.nsw.gov.au

The ServiceNSW website is maintained by the New South Wales government’s Office of Information Technology (OIT). This Privacy Policy applies to all the publicly accessible pages on the ServiceNSW site located at [http://www.nsw.gov.au](http://www.nsw.gov.au/) and to all the publicly accessible pages listed in the search results web pages. OIT does not have any responsibility for the privacy policies or practices of third party sites linked to the ServiceNSW site. If you have any questions about the ServiceNSW site, or the application of this Privacy Policy or a request for access to information held please contact Trevor Waters, Manager ServiceNSW, Level 3, 1 Prince Albert Road, Sydney, phone 9236 7702, fax 9236 7732, and email[ trevor.waters@ditm.nsw.gov.au](mailto:trevor.waters@ditm.nsw.gov.au)

Users of the ServiceNSW site are entitled to expect that any information as a result of that use will be treated within the terms of the New South Wales government’s privacy responsibilities and obligations. The New South Wales government’s privacy practices are regulated by the New South Wales [ Privacy and Personal Information Protection Act 1988](http://www.austlii.edu.au/au/legis/nsw/consol_act/papipa1998464/).

**What information do we collect?**  
When you look at the pages on the ServiceNSW site, our computers automatically record information that identifies, for each page accessed:

  * the IP (Internet Protocol) address of the machine which has accessed it;
  * your top-level domain name (for example .com, .gov, .au, .uk etc.);
  * the address of your server;
  * the date and time of your visit to the site;
  * the pages accessed and documents downloaded; 
  * the previous site visited; and
  * the type of browser and operating system you have used.



Cookies are not used from the Service NSW site to collect information.

**How do we use the information collected?**  
The information collected during each visit is aggregated with similar logged information and published in reports in order for OIT to identify patterns of usage of the site. This will assist us in improving the ServiceNSW site and the services offered on it.

OIT will not disclose or publish information that identifies individual machines, or potentially identifies sub-groupings of addresses, without consent or otherwise in accordance with the New South Wales[ Privacy and Personal Information Protection Act 1988](http://www.austlii.edu.au/au/legis/nsw/consol_act/papipa1998464/).

**What exceptions are there to this rule?**  
OIT will collect, use and disclose more extensive information than stated above in the following circumstances:

  * unauthorised attempts to access files which are not published ServiceNSW pages;
  * unauthorised tampering or interference with files published on the ServiceNSW site;
  * unauthorised attempts to index the contents of the ServiceNSW site by other sites;
  * attempts to intercept messages of other users of the ServiceNSW site;
  * communications which are defamatory, abusive, vilify individuals or groups or which give rise to a suspicion that an offence is being committed; and
  * attempts to otherwise compromise the security of the web server, breach the laws of the State of New South Wales or Commonwealth of Australia, or interfere with the enjoyment of the ServiceNSW site by other users.



OIT reserves the right to make disclosures to relevant authorities where the use of the ServiceNSW site raises a suspicion that an offence is being, or has been, committed.

In the event of an investigation, OIT will provide access to data to any law enforcement agency that may exercise a warrant to inspect our logs.

**Is the information stored securely?**  
Yes. Information collected is stored in an appropriately secure format and held by DITM for archival purposes. When the information is no longer required for the purposes for which it was collected it is deleted.

**What will we do with information provided in Feedback?  
**OIT provides feedback facilities on the ServiceNSW site to allow users to provide input into the future development of the site and to comment on the provision of service by the Office.

The provision of personal details on feedback is optional.

Users may provide personal details for the purpose of receiving a reply to their feedback. This information will only be used for the purpose for which it was provided. We will not add your email address or name to any mailing list.

OIT may publish aggregated information about feedback on the site, to the extent that it does not identify or cannot be used to identify individual users.

**Who else has access to information within the Department?  
**OIT captures this information on its own computers. Access to the raw data is restricted to a limited number of officers in OIT for the purpose of analysis and to report on the success of ServiceNSW in meeting OIT’s communication and access objectives. The officers who can access this data are the members of the connect.nsw team of the Office of Information Technology. If you would like to access information held about you by OIT please contact Trevor Waters, ServiceNSW Manager, Level 3, 1 Prince Albert Road, Sydney, phone 9236 7702, fax 9236 7732 and email [ trevor.waters@ditm.nsw.gov.au](mailto:trevor.waters@ditm.nsw.gov.au)

**Will this policy be updated over time?  
**Due to the developing nature of privacy principles for online communication, this policy may be modified or expanded in light of new developments or issues that may arise from time to time. The amended policy will be posted to this site and will operate from the time it is posted.
