**Information we collect**   


|    
  
|   
| 

We do not share information about you with other companies. We automatically collect your IP address, but not your email address when you visit our site. We use your IP address to help diagnose problems with our server, and to administer our Web site. 

Our site uses order forms for customers to request information, products, and services. We collect your contact information (like email address) and financial information (like credit card numbers). Contact information from the order form is used to send orders and information about our company to you. You may opt-out of receiving future mailings; see the How to remove your name from our mailing or email list section below. Financial information that is collected is used to bill you for products and services.

**About cookies and how we use them**   


|    
**Back to Top**   
|   
| 

A cookie is a small file that a Web site puts on your hard drive so that it can remember something about you at a later time. Cookies make it easier for you to use our site. 

We use cookies for two reasons. First, a cookie is generated that allows you to retain contents in your shopping bag while you are checking out other areas on our site. When you are done, the gear in your bag is still there for you to buy. We also use cookies to collect aggregate data about what areas of our site are the most popular and least popular. This allows us to give you more of what you want and less of what you don't want. In keeping with our commitment to your privacy, we do not analyze this data on the personal level.

**How we use email**   


|    
**Back to Top**   
|   
|    


If you want to hear from us, you have to tell us you want to hear from us and provide a way for us to contact you. You are not at risk of being deluged with spam. If you have filled out the registered for Patagonia email and entered your email address, we'll send you a message when something special happens at Patagonia.com - a major site update or new season's products qualifies as something special. Any time you provide us with your email address, such as when you email us with a question or comment, you will be given the choice to opt out of receiving Patagonia emails. You might receive one or two emails from us in a month. If you currently receive our emails and do not wish to continue receiving them, please see the How to remove your name from our mailing or email list section below.

**Secure shopping at Patagonia**   


| 

**Back to Top**

|   
| 

Ordering online at patagonia.com is safe and easy! Protecting your order information is a priority, so we use VeriSign's Secure Sockets Layer (SSL) technology. SSL encrypts ALL of your personal information including credit card number name and address to avoid the decoding of that information by anyone other than Patagonia. If you are using a Netscape browser, check the security of your connection by looking at the lower left-hand corner of your browser window after accessing the server. For Internet Explorer, check the little boxes near the lower right-hand corner. If you see an unbroken key or a closed lock (depending upon your browser), then SSL is active. Please refer to the following chart to see which security symbols are used in you browser.

**Netscape 1.x, 2.x, 3.x** |  |

You can also double-check by looking at the URL line of your browser. When accessing a secure server, the first characters of the site address will change from "http" to "https". 

[]() We have implemented the strongest security available for Patagonia.com from VeriSign. VeriSign software enables the use of digital certificates which confirm that your browser in communicating securely with Patagonia.com. You may click on the VeriSign logo to the right to verify that your browser is communicating securely with Patagonia.com. 

Some versions of browsers and some firewalls don't permit communication through secure servers. If you are unable to connect to a secure server or would rather place your order by phone, please call us at 1-800-638-6464
