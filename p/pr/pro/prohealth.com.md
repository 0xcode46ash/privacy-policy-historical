**How We Collect Information On You, and Why We Collect It**

Each computer is set up with a unique number called an "IP address." We use this number to help diagnose problems with our server and to administer our web site. Your IP address is used to gather broad demographic information, which we use to improve our site so that our users evolving needs are met. The IP address is in no way linked to the user's personally identifiable information.

We use "cookies" which are small text files that are stored on your hard drive, which is a standard in Internet activity. Cookies contain information about you and your preferences. They allow us to deliver content, specific to your interests, and allow us to save your password so you don't have to re-enter it each time you visit our site. They also allow us to keep track of items in your shopping cart for checkout.

If you choose not to accept cookies in your browser you will still be able to access all functional areas of the site, however, you will not be able to purchase products using our online store.

Our site's shopping cart requires a user to provide billing information (like your name and credit card number) and shipping information (like your address and zip code). Billing information is used to bill your credit card for purchases made on our site. A third party intermediary is used to process credit card transactions. Customers credit card information is forwarded to this third party solely for the purpose of processing the credit card transaction.

In addition, during checkout users have the option of receiving our newsletter. Users may "opt-out" (choose not to) of receiving future mailings; see the choice/opt-out section below.

We use the collected data to tailor the visitor's experience at our site, showing them content that we think they might be interested in, and displaying the content according to their preferences.

Our Refer- a - friend program allows customers to refer friends to our web site and receive a $10 credit towards there next purchase. Information (i.e. email, name, zip code and phone number) is required to credit and track customers. This information is used to send a one time email to your friend. Friend's emails are not kept or used for any other purpose.

**Third-Party Relationships**

prohealth.com has a relationship with outside parties in order to provide management of our email lists and various services for prohealth.com's users. prohealth.com does not share any personally identifiable information with any third party. This third party gathers customers email address as well as areas of interest. All information gathered by this third party is used strictly for the purpose of mailing requested emails. 

A third party intermediary is used to process credit card transactions. Customers credit card information is forwarded to this third party solely for the purpose of processing the credit card transaction.

Lastly, a third party is used to manage our affiliates. Customers who wish to be an affiliate with prohealth.com will register with this third party. Information such as company name, website, address, and banking information is collected and used for the purpose of tracking sales and paying commission for each affiliate. When an affiliate signs up for our affiliate program they will be directed to our this third party's site. Please note that when you click on one of these links, you are clicking to another website. We encourage you to read the privacy statements of these linked sites as their privacy practices may differ from ours.

**WHAT ARE COOKIES? WHY DO WE USE THEM?**

Cookies are small files stored by your browser in your computer when you visit our Site. We use cookies to improve our Site and make it easier to use. Cookies permit us to recognize users and avoid repetitive requests for the same information. We mostly use "session cookies" that are automatically deleted after each visit. Cookies from our Site can't be read by other sites. Most browsers will accept cookies until you change browser settings to refuse them. 

**HOW DO WE PROTECT INFORMATION WE COLLECT?**

We offer secure web pages to collect certain kinds of user information and we store certain kinds of data in encrypted form. We follow reasonable technical and management practices to help protect the confidentiality, security and integrity of data stored on our system. While no computer system is completely secure, we believe the measures implemented by our Site reduce the likelihood of security problems to a level appropriate to the type of data involved. 

**HOW LONG DO WE KEEP USER INFORMATION?**

We generally keep user data on our server or in our archives for about one hundred eighty (180) days. We may alter this practice according to changing requirements. For example, we may delete some data if needed to free up storage space. We may keep other data for longer periods if the law requires it. In addition, information posted in a public forum could stay in the public domain indefinitely. 

Data management requests are administered in an orderly manner to the extent feasible and within our direct control. Note: we have greater control over recently collected data than for archived data. Once data is removed from the system and archived, it may not be feasible to accommodate specific requests. In those cases, our general data retention policy applies.
