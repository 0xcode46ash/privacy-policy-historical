The need for a responsible privacy statement from any major website has become apparent. Recent media reports about online spying stress the importance of privacy. We refer you to stories on ABCNews' page including:  [European Officials Suspect the United States is Spying on Its Allies](http://more.abcnews.go.com/sections/world/DailyNews/echelon_990709.html),  [U.S. Spy Budget Revealed](http://more.abcnews.go.com/sections/us/cia1015/index.html) and [Windows Security Under Fire: Company Discovers Extra Key to Microsoft Encryption](http://more.abcnews.go.com/sections/tech/DailyNews/twokeys990903.html).  A quick search for "online spying" will yield many more alarming results from any major search engine.

The Internet is a great place to learn about the guitar, and just about anything else for that matter. From this website you have the opportunity to take online guitar lessons, find tablature, surf to other guitar sites and even communicate with other guitarists like yourself. It is important to us at **The Online Guitar College** that all of our visitors retain their privacy while taking advantage of all the Internet has to offer. 

Today many websites collect information about their visitors. In addition to the any personal information you may submit to the site, information may be gathered anonymously. Usually this is information does not personally identify you and may only be used for marketing or advertising purposes. This information is collected through "traffic data" and may include the use of "Cookies" "IP addresses" or other numeric codes used to identify a computer. 

**What are Cookies?**

Cookies are pieces of information that a Web site transfers to your computer's hard disk for record-keeping purposes. Cookies can make the Web more useful by storing information about your preferences on a particular site. The use of cookies is an industry standard, and many major Web sites use them to provide useful features for their customers. Cookies in and of themselves do not personally identify users, although they do identify a user's computer. Most browsers are initially set up to accept cookies. If you'd prefer, you can set yours to refuse cookies. However, you may not be able to take full advantage of a Web site if you do so. 

At times **The Online Guitar College** collects information about who is visiting our website. We never disclose any of this information to anyone outside our own website. We do not participate in programs that share information. Your information will never be sold, traded or given away. Your email address will never wind up on a mailing list because of us unless you specifically request we add you to our own list. 

**What About my Personal Information?**

**The Online Guitar College** does not collect personal information from you.  If you ever sign up for a mailing list or fill out a survey for us you only need to give us an email address.  All other information is voluntary. However, chat sessions, ICG discussions, newsgoups and our interactive forum are all considered public and you are responsible for your actions in these areas.  You should be aware that any personal information you share may be available to others outside our organization.  You should always be careful when sharing personal information in this manner.

**The Online Guitar College** adheres to the following **Privacy Principles:**

  1. **Principle 1**  
Any information collected is used only in an effort to improve the quality of this website and related projects not limited to other websites, future CD-Roms and books or videos.
  2. **Principle 2**  
**The Online Guitar College** will not send you any unsolicited information, including email, unless you somehow manage to abuse our website or our visitors. We will never send you unsolicited email that includes commercial offers or advertisements. 



What this means to you is that we are committed to protecting your privacy and developing a website that gives you a powerful, safe, online experience without any hassles.
