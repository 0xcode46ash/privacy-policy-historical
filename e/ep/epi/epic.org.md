### Hot Topics and New Resources

  * [**LSSI**](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/intl/lssi.html). Information on the new Spanish law on e-commerce. (October 2002)
  * **[Video Surveillance](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/surveillance/).** Provides links to numerous supplemental resources, guidelines, and news stories covering surveillance in Washington, DC and the United States in general, as well as internationally. (June 2002)
  * **[Data Retention](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/intl/data_retention.html).** Information about various data retention proposals in the European Union (EU). Some resources are available in French and Spanish. (May 2002)
  * **[CPNI (Customer Proprietary Network Information)](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/cpni/).** EPIC has made available a new resource on CPNI and the history of the opt-in/opt-out debate surrounding the use and collection of customer data by telephone companies. (January 2002)



### Privacy by Topic: The A to Z's of Privacy

  * [**Air Travel Privacy**](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/faa/). Materials on passenger profiling and other related proposals being proposed by the White House.
  * **[Attorney General's Guidelines](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/fbi/)**. Information about the ability of the FBI to investigate Americans.
  * **[Biometrics](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/biometrics/)**. Information about biometric technologies and privacy implications.
  * **Cable TV Records**. Text of the [Cable TV Privacy Act of 1984](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/cable_tv/ctpa.html).
  * [**Caller ID**](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/caller_id/). Includes FCC rules, court cases and international materials.
  * [**Children's Privacy**](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/kids/). Bills introduced in the House and the Senate last session to restrict collection of personal information about children for marketing purposes.
  * [**Conferences**](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/privacy_resources_faq.html#Privacy_Conferences). List of upcoming conferences on privacy.
  * [**Cookies**](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/internet/cookies/). What *are* those nasty little things, and who's been putting them on your hard drive? Check our cookies page for a list of good resources.
  * **Copyright and Privacy**. NII [ Copyright Protection Act](http://thomas.loc.gov/cgi-bin/query/z?c104:S.1284:) raises privacy issues. Comments of the [Digital Future Coalition](http://www.dfc.org/dfc1/Archives/n2/copyrigh.html) and the [Consumer Project on Technology](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/copyright/cpt_ltr.txt).
  * [**Counter-terrorism**](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/terrorism/). Congressional proposals to increase wiretapping, revise government guidelines on investigating domestic political groups and obtain easier access to credit reports and transactional information.
  * **[CPNI](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/cpni/)**. Customer Proprietary Network Information (CPNI) and the history of the opt-in/opt-out debate surrounding the use and collection of customer data by telephone companies.
  * **Credit Reports**. Text of the Fair Credit Reporting Act, FCRA facts from the Federal Trade Commission and information on contacting the credit bureaus on the [EPIC Federal Trade Commission](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/internet/FTC/default.html#fcra) page.
  * [**Cryptography Policy**](https://web.archive.org/crypto/). Information on the Clipper Chip, Key Escrow and other pending issues related to cryptography policy.
  * [**Data Retention**](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/intl/data_retention.html). Information about various data retention proposals in the European Union (EU). Some resources are available in French and Spanish.
  * **Digital Cash**. Federal Reserve Chairman Alan Greenspan delivered a major [speech](http://www.federalreserve.gov/BOARDDOCS/SPEECHES/19970307.htm) discussing privacy and electronic payment systems, noting the need for policies that "avoid the risk of a gradual, long-term erosion of privacy." Also, check out an [earlier speech](http://www.federalreserve.gov/BOARDDOCS/SPEECHES/19960919.htm) by Greenspan on the important role of cash transactions for consumers.
  * [**Digital Rights Management (DRM)**](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/drm/default.html). The technologies used to secure digital files will enable profiling of consumers and prevent anonymous consumption of content.
  * [**Direct Marketing and Junk Mail**](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/junk_mail/). Information on Avrahami case and other attempts to protect personal information and stem the flow of junk mail.
  * **[Driving Records](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/drivers/)**. The Driver's Privacy Protection Act (DPPA) sets out a framework for protecting DMV records.
  * **Electronic Mail**. "[Electronic Interaction in the Workplace: Monitoring, Retrieving and Storing Employee Communications in the Internet Age](http://www.morganlewis.com/art61499.htm)," a comprehensive analysis (from the *employer* perspective) by Mark S. Dichter and Michael S. Burkhardt (1999). For a different perspective, see the Ontario [Privacy Principles for E-mail](http://www.ipc.on.ca/english/pubpres/hilights/email.htm).
  * [**ENUM**](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/enum/default.html). "Electronic Numbering" is a system that allows a user to obtain contact information, such as a telephone, fax, and e-mail address with a single phone number.
  * [**Face Recognition**](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/facerecognition/). Information and resources on facial recognition technology.
  * [**Federal Trade Commission**](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/internet/ftc/). **** Consumer protector or industry lapdog? What the FTC has done (or not done) on protecting privacy on the net and with credit reports.
  * **[Gender and Electronic Privacy](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/gender/)**. Information on privacy-invasive behaviors that tend to be targeted at women, including cyberstalking, pretexting, and video voyeurism.
  * **[Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/glba/)**. Information on a 1999 federal law that establishes some protections against pretexting and limitations on data sharing for banks, insurance, and brokerage companies.
  * [**International Privacy**](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/intl/). Information on international privacy laws and standards, including the recently enacted EU Data Protection Directive and proposals for Canada and the US. Also see the [Privacy International Home Page](http://www.privacyinternational.org/)
  * [**Internet Privacy**](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/internet/). EPIC reports, Email privacy, America Online, the Federal Trade Commission, Spam and more.
  * [**LSSI**](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/intl/lssi.html). Information on the 2002 Spanish law on e-commerce. Many resources and articles are available in Spanish.
  * [**Medical Records**](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/medical/). Information on current problems and pending legislation.
  * [**Microsoft Palladium**](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/consumer/microsoft/palladium.html). Information about Microsoft's plans to embed Digital Rights Management into hardware and software.
  * [**Microsoft Passport**](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/consumer/microsoft/). Information about Microsoft's Passport, Hailstorm, and .NET systems. Also see the **[Project Liberty Page](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/authentication/projectliberty.html)**.
  * [**Microsoft Passport Investigation Docket Page**](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/consumer/microsoft/passport.html). Information about Federal Trade Commission and European Commission investigations of Microsoft Passport.
  * [**National ID Cards**](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/id_cards/). Pending proposals in Congress, reports, and international updates.
  * **New Technologies**. ["Big Brother Goes High Tech"](http://mediafilter.org/caq/CAQ56brother.html) by Dave Banisar. A summary of new surveillance technologies. Information on EPIC and Privacy International's annual conference on [Advanced Surveillance Technologies](http://www.privacy.org/pi/conference/).
  * **Online Databases**. What Big Brother holds on you. Information on [IRS employee browsing](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/databases/irs/), the [SSA PEBES controversy](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/databases/ssa/), [Filegate](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/databases/fbi/filegate/) and [FBI Databases](http://www.fbi.gov/2000/2000newl.htm).
  * [**Personal and Consumer Information**](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/consumer/). How personal information about you is stored, transferred and used.
  * **[Profiling](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/profiling/)**. How an entire industry dedicated to building dossiers on humans collects and exploits personal information from sources such as product warranty cards, product return forms, calls to customer service departments, surveys, and sweepstakes.
  * **[Project Liberty](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/authentication/projectliberty.html)**. A developing identification and authentication system that is similar to **[Microsoft Passport](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/consumer/microsoft/)**.
  * [**Public Opinion**](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/survey/default.html). What the American public thinks about privacy.
  * **[Public Records and Privacy](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/publicrecords/)**. Public records are being used by profiling companies to build dossiers on citizens. These dossiers are often sold to law enforcement agencies.
  * [**Social Security Numbers**](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/ssn/). Information on how to protect your SSN, the text of the 1974 Privacy Act and various court cases on disclosure.
  * [**SPAM -- Unsolicited E-Mail**](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/junk_mail/spam/). Resources on SPAM and pending proposals to curb it.
  * **[Student and School Records Privacy](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/student/)**. Resources on the Federal Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA), Protection of Pupil Rights Amendment (PPRA), drug testing and school searches. 
  * **[Telemarketing](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/telemarketing/)**. Information about telemarketing, federal and state attempts to restrict telemarketing, and what you can do to end annoying sales calls to your home.
  * [**Video Rental Records.**](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/vppa/) Information on the Video Privacy Protection Act.
  * [**Video Surveillance**](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/surveillance/). Provides links to numerous supplemental resources, guidelines, and news stories covering surveillance in Washington, DC and the United States in general, as well as internationally. Also see Privacy International's extensive [page](http://www.privacyinternational.org/issues/cctv/index.html) on Closed Circuit TV and video surveillance.
  * [**Wiretapping and Electronic Surveillance**](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/wiretap/). Updates on the digital telephony law, statistics, and other material on electronic surveillance.
  * [**Workplace Privacy**](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/workplace/). Workers of the world are exposed to numerous forms of surveillance while earning a living. This page contains updates on workplace privacy law, a summary of developments in the field, and resources for employees and employers.



### General Privacy Information

  * **Surfer Beware.** EPIC report on consumer privacy and the Internet. "[Surfer Beware: Personal Privacy and the Internet](https://web.archive.org/reports/surfer-beware.html)" is based on a review of the privacy practices of top Internet web sites. The report examines the current state of privacy policies on the Internet and includes recommendations to protect online privacy.
  * [**Practical Privacy Tools**](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/tools.html). Information on how to get PGP and other encryption programs, anonymous remailers and other privacy enhancing technologies.  
 
  * EPIC's [**Online Guide to Privacy Resources.**](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/privacy_resources_faq.html)  
 
  * Comprehensive overview of [state privacy laws](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/consumer/states.html) (from the [_Privacy Journal_](http://www.privacyjournal.net/)[)](http://www.townonline.com/privacyjournal).
  * [**Resources for the 2000-2001 High School Debate Topic**](https://web.archive.org/web/20021207065600id_/http://www.epic.org/privacy/debate_resources.html). This is also a good general privacy resources page.
