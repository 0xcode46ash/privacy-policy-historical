# Privacy

### How is client information used?

iVvy may use client information to provide its clients with system information or information about new or upgraded products. ivvy may also share client information with selected partners or resellers, for example, to provide clients with information about products, which might be of interest to the client, or to enable the client to take advantage of special deals or partner programs.

### Modification of client information

If clients needs to or wishes to modify any of their information, including unsubscribing from any mail lists, they can update by logging into the client section of the iVvy website (http://www.thewebconsole.com). It is especially important you keep your contact details up-to-date.

### Security

iVvy will protect the confidentiality of its client's information, account information and personal communications to the fullest extent possible and consistent with the law and the legitimate interests of iVvy, its partners, its resellers, its employees and other clients of iVvy. To protect the loss, misuse, and alteration of information that is collected from clients, iVvy has appropriate physical, electronic, and managerial security procedures in place.

When purchasing from iVvy your financial details are passed through a secure server using the latest 128-bit SSL (secure sockets layer) encryption technology.128-bit SSL encryption is approximated to take at least one trillion years to break, and is the industry standard. If you have any questions regarding our privacy policy, please contact us on the details below.

### Disclosure of client information and communications

iVvy will not otherwise disclose its client's personal and account information unless iVvy has reason to believe that disclosing such information is necessary to identify, make contact with, or bring legal action against someone who may be causing harm or interfering with the rights or property of iVvy, iVvy's clients, or others, or where iVvy has a good faith belief that the law requires such disclosure.

iVvy also will not, except for reasons stated below, disclose to third parties the contents of any electronic mail or other electronic communications that iVvy Â stores or transmits for its clients. The circumstances under which iVvy will disclose such electronic client communications are when:

It is necessary in order to provide service to the client;  
It is necessary to protect the legitimate interests of iVvy and its clients;  
It is required to cooperate with interception orders, warrants, or other legal processes that iVvy determines in its sole discretion to be valid and enforceable; and  
It is necessary to provide to a law enforcement agency when the contents are inadvertently obtained by Bloomtools and appear to pertain to the commission of a crime.
