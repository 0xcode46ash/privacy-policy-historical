### Privacy policy

The protection of your data and privacy is of special concern to us. We use private data, which is collected due to your visit on this website, corresponding to the German Federal Data Protection Act and the German Telemedia Act. Your data will only be used in the framework of the MOOC-contest and will not be passed on to third parties. 

### Collection and usage of personal data

Personal data (e.g. your email address) will only be collected on this website if you enter them. The iversity GmbH does not hand over your email adress to third parties. Your e-mail address will be used to send you, at reasonable time intervals, an e-mail newsletter. However, you shall always have the option to unsubscribe this newsletter, for example by a simple e-mail to [support@iversity.org](https://web.archive.org/web/20130424174246id_/http%3A//www.iversity.org/support@iversity.org) or by clicking the appropriate link provided in each newsletter. 

### Logfiles

When websites are visited via iversity.org, access data is submitted by your web browser, which is stored by the iversity GmbH in the automated log files, known as server logs. These log files contain the following information: 

  * IP address of the computer
  * date, time, status, amount of data transferred and the website from which you came to the requested page (referrer)
  * product and version information of the browser (user agent), operating system, language settings.



The IP addresses automatically collected via these log files are anonymized and used if needed, in this anonymous form, for the improvement of iversity and for security reasons, to be able to trace cases of abuse of the service offered by iversity GmbH. You are entitled to prohibit, at any point in time, this kind of use of the log files via e-mail or by mail. 

A compilation of this data with other sources of data, particularly with the personal data of the user, is not performed. The iversity GmbH, however, reserves the right to inspect existing logs for the last known IP address of the user, in the case of a definite legal offence or contract violation by the user. This serves mainly to protect the users of iversity and the security of user data on iversity. 

### Transfer to third parties

Handling and usage of your data for additional purpose, i.e. consultation or advertising, will only take place with your explicit approval. Inquiries from state institutions and authorities and the transfer of personal data to them are only handled strictly within the framework of binding national law. 

### Right of information and public procedure catalog

According to §34 BDSG, you have unlimited rights, free of charge, to information about the personal data referring to yourself which we have saved, as well as the right (according to §35 BDSG) to delete or block inaccurate data. On request, we are prepared to send you written information whether we - and if so, which - personal data we have stored about you. As far as possible, we will take the suitable measures to update or correct your data stored with us at short notice. Just send us an email. 

### Objection

You can object to the use of your personal data at any time. No additional fee besides the basic transmission costs will be charged. 

### Links

Our website contains links to the websites of other providers, for which we assume no liability with respect to content and which are not obliged to our privacy policies. If we provide links to other websites we try to make sure that these website are aligned with our values. Nevertheless we cannot influence their privacy policies. If you visit another website please inform yourself about their privacy policies. 

### Security

We have technical and organisational security measures in place to ensure the security of your information and to protect it against deliberate or accidental manipulation loss, destruction or access by unauthorized persons. 

### Use of Facebook plugins

On websites of iversity.org plugins of the network facebook.com are used; (e.g. the "Like Button") owned by Facebook Inc., 1601 S. California Ave, Palo Alto, CA 94304 , USA. Our corresponding sites are marked with the appropriate button. If you open our web sites which include such a plugin, your browser will connect directly to the servers of Facebook. The contents of the plugin originate from Facebook Inc. and are transmitted directly to your browser and displayed accordingly on our site, without us being able to alter the contents of the plugin. 

Facebook Inc. may understand from the information transmitted to the server of Facebook Inc., which of our web sites you have visited and associate this information to a Facebook account, if you are registered on Facebook or visited a Facebook page or other pages with Facebook content recently. Also if you want to actively use Facebook plugins (eg press the Like button), the corresponding information from your browser will be directly transmitted to Facebook Inc., without us being able to exert any influence over the process. For detailed information on how and to what extent the Facebook Inc. collects, processes and uses relevant data, please refer to the privacy policies of Facebook Inc., which can be found under www.facebook.com / policy.php. There you can also find information on how to protect your privacy. To prevent Facebook Inc. from collecting information about you during the visit to our website, you can log out of Facebook before visiting our web pages and delete existing cookies from Facebook Inc. from your browser. 

### Use of Twitter plugins

On websites of iversity.org plugins of the short message service Twitter Inc., 795 Folsom St., Suite 600, San Francisco, CA 94107, USA are used ("tweet" button). When you visit our websites via the corresponding "tweet" button, the plugin establishes a direct connection between your browser and the Twitter servers. Twitter receives the information that you have visited our site using your IP address and may associate this information with your Twitter account. If you klick on the "tweet" button while you are logged into your Twitter account, you can link content on our pages with your Twitter profile. Thus it is possible for Twitter Inc. to associate a visit to our sites with your user account. iversity has no influence on the transmission and processing of any data by Twitter. For more information on data processing at Twitter, please refer to the privacy policy statement on http://twitter.com/privacy. If you want to avoid that Twitter can associate your visit to iversity.org with your Twitter account, please log out of your Twitter account before visiting iversity.org. 

### Use of Google Analytics

a) The iversity GmbH uses Google Analytics, a web analytics service provided by Google Inc. of 1600 Amphitheatre Parkway, Mountain View, CA 94043, USA ("Google"). Google Analytics also uses cookies (see section 1.2.2) which can analyze your usage of websites. The information generated by the cookie about your use of the website (visit times and referring sources, details on the system configuration and your provider, including a portion of your IP address) is transmitted to a Google server in the U.S. and stored there. 

iversity uses Google Analytics to analyze site usage anonymously. Google may also transfer this anonymous information to third parties if required by law or if third parties process this data on Google's behalf. Google will not associate your IP address with any other data held by Google. 

By using this site you agree to the processing of your data by Google in the manner and for the purpose as described above. However, you may refuse to accept new cookies by adjusting your browser settings (see above). You may also deactivate Google Analytics by installing a so called browser-add-on, which is provided by Google under the following address: <http://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout?hl=en>. 

More information on Google Inc. and Google Analytics can be found here: [http://www.google.com](http://www.google.com/). Google’s privacy policy can be found [here](http://www.google.com/intl/de/privacy/privacy-policy.html).
