# InternetCorkboard Newsletter

  
We receive and may keep information you gave us because you decided to register on one of our, or one of our affiliate’s websites, or because you decided to take part in a special promotion or fill out any other form on one of our, our one of our affiliate’s websites. This information may include (1) your e-mail address, (2) your name, (3) your mailing address, (4) your telephone number, (5) your date of birth, and (6) your gender. This information may also include any other personal information or unique preference information you choose to provide us.

We may receive information about you from other sources. This information may help us to improve our ability to service your needs on a personalized basis. It helps us to learn about your shopping preferences and also helps us let you know about new offers from us or our affiliates. We may use this information to tell you about great offers that we think you might like.

  
We have security measures in place designed to protect the security of your information such as private network connections, firewalls, and data encryption. However, there is no such thing as complete security on the Internet or otherwise. The Company uses industry standard security techniques on our websites to help protect against the loss, misuse or modification of information we’ve collected from you. When you access your personal information or transmit personally identifiable data to us, that information is stored on servers that we have protected from unauthorized access or intrusion. While we strive to protect your information, the Company cannot ensure or warrant the security of any information you transmit to us. You acknowledge and assume this risk when communicating with the Company, its partners and affiliates.

  
We want you to be aware that certain tools exist to help us service and recognize you. For your convenience, to help us personalize your experience or better serve your needs, we may receive and store certain types of information when you visit one of our websites. You may be familiar with the term “cookies,” which are unique alphanumeric identifiers that store your personal information. These cookies are placed on your computer when your web browser accesses our websites. We use this information to educate us on things such as how you navigate to and around our websites, product browsing, content accessing, and purchasing data. If you choose, there are utilities available for purchase from independent software providers to install on your computer as well as tools/preferences you can choose through most web browsers to make website visits anonymous. Of course, cookies let you have a more satisfying website experience, so we recommend that you leave them turned on.

Company may be required to collect and submit certain information required by the government, such as a completed IRS Form W-9. This information, which may include your taxpayer identification number, will not be used, shared, transferred, or sold for marketing purposes. 

  
We share information we collect from all points of contact within the Company network, including with our parent company and with any other subsidiaries of our parent. The information you give us and information about your transaction may be combined with other personally identifiable information available from our records and other sources. We use this information to offer you products, services, offers and benefits that may be of interest to you.

We may share your information with carefully selected vendors, business partners and other organizations which are not part of the Company. These companies and organizations use the information we provide them to assist us in serving your needs. These disclosures may be made to help us enforce or apply existing terms and conditions and/or other agreements or to protect the rights, property or safety of our websites, our users or others. We may also share your information with carefully selected advertisers and marketers for various marketing purposes, including their on-line and/or off-line programs or services.

We may share information with governmental agencies or other companies assisting us in fraud prevention or investigation. We may do so when (1) permitted or required by law, (2) trying to protect against or prevent actual or potential fraud or unauthorized transactions, or (3) investigating fraud that has already taken place. In all cases, this information is not provided to these entities for marketing purposes. 

Should the Company choose to sell or transfer business assets, it is possible that the information we possess may be transferred as part of that transaction. The Company may decide to retain a copy of the information post sale or transfer.

  
We recognize the unique privacy concerns regarding children. We are committed to carefully protecting privacy where children are involved no matter the point of contact. In particular, our intention is to fully comply with the Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA). Per COPPA guidelines, we do not knowingly collect personally identifiable information from children under the age of 13. If a parent or guardian is made aware that a child under the age of 13 has provided us with personally identifiable information through any point of contact, we ask to be contacted at privacy@internetcorkboard.com and we will delete the information about the child referenced from our files.
