# Corporate Information

**

[](https://www.esrb.org/confirm/capcom-confirmation.htm)

**

Capcom and its subsidiaries respect the privacy rights of online visitors and is committed to protecting your collected information. This Privacy Statement guides how we collect, store and use the information that you provide.

This Privacy Statement, and the certification seal located on the main page, confirms that Capcom is a valid licensee and participating member in the Entertainment Software Rating Boards Privacy Online Program: ESRB Privacy Online. To protect your privacy, we have voluntarily undertaken this initiative. ESRB Privacy Online has reviewed all of our websites as having met the established online information collection and use practices. As part of this privacy program, we are subject to frequent audits of our sites and other enforcement and accountability mechanisms administered independently by ESRB Privacy Online.

ESRB Privacy Online is a third-party seal provider whose mission is to protect consumers online privacy and make the Internet a secure, reliable, and private place to share information and conduct business. ESRB Privacy Online promotes and enforces established principles and guidelines for fair information practices that include requirements of full disclosure, notice, and informed consent.

Whenever you visit a website that displays the ESRB Privacy Online certification seal, you can expect to be notified of:

>   * Exactly what personal information may be collected and by what means. 
>   * The consequences, if any, resulting from your refusal to provide personal information. 
>   * Whether a company uses cookies, tracks IP Addresses or uses other passive means to collect information. 
>   * Who, if anyone, is collecting your personal information and how will it be used. 
>   * With whom, if anyone, will your personal information be shared. 
>   * What choices you have with regard to the use of your personal information. 
>   * How you can access and, if necessary, change the personal information a company collects and maintains. 
>   * How, if at all, information is collected from Children under 13. 
>   * How a company ensures data security, quality, and integrity. 
>   * Where to direct questions or file complaints.

2\. What happens if you refuse to provide personal information?

**

We will only collect information to the extent deemed reasonably necessary to fulfill our legitimate business objectives. Of course you never have to answer any of the questions we ask you, but refusal to do so may limit the services we can offer you: registration, newsletter, sweepstakes, posting messages, and online purchasing.

**

3\. What passive collection methods do we use?

**

Capcom uses cookies on its website. A "cookie" is a small bit of record-keeping information that websites often store on a users computer. Cookies do not include peoples names, e-mail addresses, or other information that is personally identifiable; they are used only to identify the computer. The use of cookies is standard on the Internet and many major websites use them. You can, however, set your browser to notify you when you receive a cookie, giving you the chance to decide whether or not to accept it. To purchase online, however, you must accept cookies.

We do not keep track of Internet Protocol (IP) addresses.

The information we collect is used to improve the products and services we provide to you on our website. For example, we may use the information we collect to notify you about special offers or an opportunity that we believe you would be interested in.

5\. With whom does Capcom share my personal information?

None of your information will be shared with outside third parties.

**

If you have opted-in and at a later date you choose to have your name removed from our e-mail database, you can do so by selecting the unsubscribe link at the bottom of the email or sending a remove request to [megamail@capcom.com](mailto:megamail@capcom.com). You may also call (408) 774-0400.

7\. How do I access my personal information?

**

If for any reason you are concerned that the personal information maintained by us is not correct, or should you wish to have your information removed from our records, please email [megamail@capcom.com](mailto:megamail@capcom.com), or call (408) 774-0400, and we will be happy to review, update, correct or remove information as appropriate.

8\. Does Capcom collect personal information from children?

**_

Children 12 years old and under

_

We require children 12 years old and under to obtain prior parental consent before we collect any information from that child. If you indicate that you are 12 years old or under, we will ask you to print out a permission form from our site, which must be signed and returned by your parent or guardian before we will accept information from you. It is also our policy not to require a child to disclose more information than is reasonably necessary to participate in an activity as a condition of participation.

_

Children 13 to 17 years old

_

We will not collect personal information from children over 12 and under 18 without notifying a parent or guardian via email.

_

Parental Access

_

If at any time, you wish to view or correct your childs personal information; have Capcom discontinue further use of your childs information or communication with your child; and/or, revoke your earlier consent to allow Capcom to collect your childs information, please contact us by phone (408) 774-0400 or by email: [megamail@capcom.com](mailto:megamail@capcom.com). We will be happy to review and update your childs information as appropriate.
