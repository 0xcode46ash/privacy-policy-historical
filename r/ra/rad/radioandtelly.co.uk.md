# Radio and Telly Website Privacy Policy | Radio & Telly UK

Our Privacy Policy & Cookie Information

### Policy Overview

We respect your privacy, and do not give or sell any information that you may provide us with to third parties.

By using this website, you agree to accept our Privacy Policy. Be aware that our Privacy Policy may be subject to change. Changes to our Privacy Policy will appear on this web page. 

### Information gathered from visitors

In common with other websites, log files are stored on the web server saving details such as the visitor’s IP address, browser type, referring page and time of visit.

Visitors that wish to interact with site’s discussion forum will be required to register. Registering results in the visitor’s email address, a username and password will be stored on the web server. Additional information can be entered optionally by the user to form of a user profile.

An email newsletter service is offered by this site. Should a visitor elect to join the mailing list, their email address, plus email preferences, will be stored on the server.

Cookies may be used to remember visitor preferences when interacting with the website.

### How the Information is used

Visitor information as outlined on this page is used to monitor and improve the performance of our websites and services, and enhance the visitor’s experience when using the website.

Where a visitor opts in to our email mailing list or registers with our discussion forum, the email address may be used to provide visitors with emails relating to our services, as well as news updates and additional information.

Email addresses will not be sold, rented or leased to third parties.

If requested, we may submit your information to local or government authorities. 

### Visitor Options

If you have subscribed to one of our services, you may unsubscribe by following the instructions which are included in email that you receive.

You may be able to block cookies via your browser settings but this may prevent you from accessing certain features of the website, such as some forum functionality.

### Content submitted to this site

To offer a better service to site visitors, we may republish extracts of emails sent to this website, comments made in our discussion forum, comments made to blog posts, or other messages sent to us electronically. Should we elect to republish content, we will not disclose any user’s email address, telephone number or postal address.

If you do not want us to republish any extracts from content or correspondence submitted to us, then this should be made clear in the body text at the time of submission.

### Cookies

Cookies are small digital signature files that are stored by your web browser that allow your preferences to be recorded when visiting the website. They may be used to track your return visits to the website.

Operating costs for running this website are funded by site advertising using affiliate networks. Some advertisement placements on this website make use of third-party cookies from one or more advertising networks. These cookies use anonymous data to improve the advertising experience for visitors, for tracking purposes, and to attribute advertising impressions to this website.

You may be able to block cookies via your browser settings but this may prevent you from accessing certain features of the website, such as some forum functionality.

You can find out more about cookies and how to control and delete them here: [www.aboutcookies.org](http://www.aboutcookies.org/)

#### COOKIES USED ON THIS SITE

For your information, here is a summary of the cookies used on this site:

 **Google Analytics**

Our website uses Google Analytics to help analyse how users use our site. Google Analytics uses “cookies,” which are text files placed on your computer, to collect standard Internet log information and visitor behavior information in an anonymous form. The information generated by these cookies about your use of the website (including your IP address) is transmitted to Google. This information is then used to evaluate visitors’ use of our site and to compile statistical reports on website activity.  
We will never (and will not allow any third party to) use the statistical analytics tool to track or to collect any Personally Identifiable Information of visitors to our site. Google will not associate your IP address with any other data held by Google. Neither we nor Google will link, or seek to link, an IP address with the identity of a computer user. We will not associate any data gathered from this site with any Personally Identifiable Information from any source, unless you explicitly submit that information via a fill-in form on our website. You can find out more information on Google Analytics, and data collected by Google here: [Google Analytics Privacy](http://www.google.com/intl/en_uk/analytics/tos.html)

>  **Google Analytics Cookies: __utma, __utmb, __utmc, __utmz and __utmv**

 **Clicky**

Our website uses Clicky to help analyse how users use our site. Clicky is a web analytics platform that provides real-time data to publishers for reporting purposes. The cookies collect the following anonymous information: browser type, exit pages, ISP, language, operating system, page views, referring URL, screen resolution, time/date, pseudonymous IP address. You can find out more information on GetClicky here: [GetClicky Terms](http://getclicky.com/terms)

>  **GetClicky Cookies: __cfduid, cluid, first_pv_ and _jsuid**

 **WordPress**

We make use of the Content Management System WordPress, which collects statistics on visitors. The cookies collect the following anonymous information: browser type, language preferences, referring URL, time/date and pseudonymous IP address. More information here: [automattic.com/privacy](http://automattic.com/privacy/)

 **Our Forum**

When you create an account and log on to our forum, three cookies are created, one for username, one for password and one for your “session”. This. You can find more information on YaBB here: <http://www.yabbforum.com/about.php>

> **YaBB Forum Cookies: __cfduid, cluid, first_pv_ and _jsuid**

 **Twitter Badge**

This site makes use of social networking site Twitter, and some of our pages contain status information using a Twitter Badge Plug-in. This uses cookies to collect the following anonymous information: ad clicks, browser type, mobile carrier, page views, referring URLs, time/date, pseudonymous IP address, search queries, “tweets”, PII (device and application IDs). More information here: [twitter.com/privacy](http://twitter.com/privacy)

 **Facebook**

This site uses social networking tools for Facebook. We use Facebook Connect and Facebook Social Plug-in to offer information about our Facebook followers. This uses cookies to collect the following anonymous information: browser type, location, page views, pseudonymous IP address, “actions taken” . More information here: [www.facebook.com/policy.php](http://www.facebook.com/policy.php)

#### Cookies: Third Party Advertising

There are costs incurred in hosting an internet website and providing a service to our visitors. Our site, and its affiliated sites make use of on-site advertising to generate revenue to cover our costs. .

Many of the advertising services we use make use of cookies to collect data to ensure that adverts are relevant, and that we receive appropriate recognition for any adverts that we serve to our visitors. In this section, we outline the services that we use, but if you want more information about how advertisers make use of cookies, and your online choices, we recommend you visit the following site: [www.youronlinechoices.com/uk/your-ad-choices](http://www.youronlinechoices.com/uk/your-ad-choices)

 **DoubleClick**

We make use of advertising services operated by Google. Known as DoubleClick DART, this services uses cookies to help target advertising. The cookies collect the following anonymous information: ad serving domains, browser type, demographics, language settings, page views, time/date and pseudonymous IP address. You can find out more, and opt out, at [www.google.com/policies/privacy/ads](http://www.google.com/policies/privacy/ads/)

 **Criteo**

We make use of advertising services operated by Criteo. Criteo is an advertising network that specializes in retargeting. Criteo’s technology enables advertisers to find potential customers who have left a website and re-engage them through dynamic banner ads generated in real-time. Each ad contains relevant, product specific information determined on a user’s previous visit to an e-commerce site. You can find more information at [www.criteo.com/us/privacy-policy](http://www.criteo.com/us/privacy-policy)

>  **Criteo Cookies: oacblock, oaccap, uid, uic, tst, udc, tag, udi, evt, dis, extid and eid**

 **Quantcast**

Quantcast is a service that lets website owners see how many views they get, what age range the people viewing are likely to be in, and what income they are likely to generate. Quantcast is one of the larger audience research and behavioural advertising companies operating, and data from Quantcast is used to better target website advertising. Anonymous data collected includes: browser type, exit page, referring URL, search index and time/date. You can find more information at [www.quantcast.com/docs/display/legal/Privacy+Policy](http://www.quantcast.com/docs/display/legal/Privacy+Policy)

 **Skimlinks**

We use the services provided by Skimlinks on some of our pages. SkimLinks is an affiliate marketing platform that allows website operators such as ourselves to be able to monetise a website through placement of affiliate advertising links. Anonymous data collected includes browser type/ version, device type and pseudonymous IP address. You can find more information on Skimlinks here: [www.skimlinks.com/privacy](http://www.skimlinks.com/privacy)
