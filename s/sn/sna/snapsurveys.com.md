### Information Collected During Registration

When you download a free trial copy we collect information, such as name, email address, organization, address, and telephone number. This information is used to create your account, contact you about the services on our site for which you have expressed particular interest. It is optional for you to provide demographic information, and unique identifiers, but encouraged so we can provide a more personalized experience. If you sign up for access to any of the protected areas of this website, we will use a cookie; this cookie will contain a unique ID that we will use to identify you when you return to the site.

We do not tolerate spam, and we will not sell, rent, lease, trade, swap, market, exchange, barter, distribute, or disclose the information we collect about you.

If you wish to add a new client to your account we will collect a username, password, name, phone number and email address. We collect this information for the sole purpose of allowing you to set up a new client within your account.  We will not use this information for any other purpose. 

### Newsletter 

If you wish to subscribe to our newsletter(s), we will use your name and email address to send the newsletter to you.  Out of respect for your privacy, we provide you a way to unsubscribe.  Please see the “Choice and Opt-out” section.

### Service-related Announcements

We will send you service-related announcements on rare occasions when it is necessary to do so. For instance, if our service is temporarily suspended for maintenance, we might send you an email. 

Generally, you may not opt-out of these communications, which are not promotional in nature. If you do not wish to receive them, you have the option to close your account.

### Forum Notifications

You have the option of receiving email notifications each time someone replies to the threads or boards you add to your notify list. If you no longer wish to receive these types of notifications simply login to your forum account and modify your notifications.

### Updating/Accessing/Amending/Correcting Personally Identifiable Information

If your personally identifiable information changes, or if you no longer desire our service, you may correct, update, or delete by making the change within your account once logged in or by emailing our Customer Support at [info@snapsurveys.com](mailto:info@snapsurveys.com?subject=Privacy%20Policy%20Question). We will respond to your request to access within 30 days. 

### Data Retention

We will retain your information for as long as your account is active or as needed to provide you services. If you wish to cancel your account or request that we no longer use your information to provide you services contact us at [privacy@snapsurveys.com](mailto:privacy@snapsurveys.com?subject=Privacy%20Policy%20Question). We will retain and use your information as necessary to comply with our legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce our agreements. 

### Cookies

A cookie is a small text file generated by a web page server which is stored on a user’s computer. Cookies  help make browsing websites easier and quicker, as well as enabling interactive features and functions. 

We use cookies on this website. None of the information we store in cookies is linked to personally identifiable information you submit while on our site. 

We use both session / transient cookies and persistent cookies:

**Session cookies** are not stored on your hard drive and expire when you close your browser, or if the session has been inactive for a set period of time. 

**Persistent cookies** remain on your hard drive for a period of time. We set a persistent cookie for example to store your passwords, so you don’t have to enter them again when you next visit the website. You can view and remove persistent cookies by following directions provided in your Internet browser’s “help” file.  To find out how to do this  in a range of browsers visit **[www.aboutcookies.org](http://www.aboutcookies.org/)**

#### Third Party Cookies 

We do use third party cookies to make it easier for you to navigate and use our site. For example:

  * Our blog  which is run on WordPress, drops a persistent cookie if you leave a comment.
  * If you visit YouTube to view our videos, cookies may be dropped by YouTube's web servers. 



The use of cookies by third parties is not covered by our privacy statement. We don't have control over these sites 

**Social Media (Features) and Widgets** : Our website includes Social Media Features, such as the Facebook "Like" button and Widgets, such as the "Share this" button or interactive mini-programs that run on our site. These features may collect your IP address, which page you are visiting on our site, and may set a cookie to enable the feature to function properly. Social Media Features and Widgets are either hosted by a third party or hosted directly on our site. Your interactions with these features are governed by the privacy policy of the company providing it.

#### Website Analysis 

This site uses Google Analytics which drops a persistent cookie. Google Analytics tracks visitor traffic patterns throughout the website. The information gathered is used to produce aggregated usage statistics about our site. This information is used to evaluate overall site performance and is not linked with data about individuals. 

More information about Google Analytics use of cookies can be found here <https://developers.google.com/analytics/devguides/collection/gajs/cookie-usage>

#### **Viewing and Deleting cookies**

If you want to see the cookies that have been dropped when you visit this and other websites, you can amend your browser settings to **view, accept or reject** some or all cookies _on this and all the websites you visit_. 

The About Cookies website gives more information about types of cookie, why they are used, and how to amend the settings in your browser to view, accept or reject cookies [www.aboutcookies.org](http://www.aboutcookies.org/)

Read more about the cookies in use on this website in our [cookie guide](https://web.archive.org/cookies/).

If you have any concerns or questions about the cookies we use on our website please email us [privacy@snapsurveys.com](mailto:privacy@snapsurveys.com?subject=Cookies)

#### **Use of Cookies in Snap Online Services**

Snap WebHost, Snap FMS, Snap Poll, and the Snap Forum use cookies to maintain the logged-in state whilst customers are using these services, and are essential to the efficient operation of these services. 

**Use of Cookies in Snap Online Surveys**

Snap WebHost questionnaires do not use cookies.

Snap HTML questionnaires will use cookies if either:

  1. you check the “Try to drop a cookie” box in the Questionnaire Properties / Interview section. The purpose of this is to prevent multiple responses from the same computer.
  2. you use the Save button feature.



In the case of **[a]** no personally identifiable information is held. For **[b]** , the responses to the questionnaire to date are held which _may_ include personally identifiable data. Both uses are essential to the functioning of the particular options and both are choices made by the questionnaire designer/publisher.

### Security

The security of your personal information is important to us.  When you enter personal information within the customer login area of the site, we encrypt that information using secure socket layer technology (SSL).      
We follow generally accepted industry standards to protect the personally identifiable information submitted to us, both during transmission and once we receive it.  No method of transmission over the Internet, or method of electronic storage, is 100% secure, however.  Therefore, while we strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your personal information, we cannot guarantee its absolute security.  
If you have any questions about security on our Web site, you can email us at [info@snapsurveys.com](mailto:info@snapsurveys.com?subject=Privacy%20Policy%20Question)
