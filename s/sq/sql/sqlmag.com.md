# Legal & Privacy Notices

  
  
[PRIVACY STATEMENT](https://web.archive.org/legal.html) [ CONTENT LICENSING FAQ ](https://web.archive.org/legal_faq.html) [SITE USE AGREEMENT](https://web.archive.org/legal_use.html)   
  
  
Duke Communications International, Inc. (Duke), doing business as _Windows 2000 Magazine Network_ and _SQL Server Magazine_ , is committed to your privacy. The following paragraphs fully describe Duke's practices regarding information you provide while using our Web sites. 

Duke collects personal information about our users only during registration for site services, order forms for our publications, entry into a Duke content or sweepstakes, and through our "cookie" technology as you use the site services. 

We aggregate data collected from cookies and other places to create a demographic picture of the entire Duke audience in order to improve site services. We also share aggregate information, not individual data, with our advertisers to help them better understand our services and to monitor their advertising reach on the sites. 

Personal information such as your name, address, etc. is used to service your account and to inform you about updates to our services. We may also use this information to notify you of new products and services from Duke, its affiliates, and third parties that we believe may be of interest to you. 

Windows 2000 Magazine Network works with a third party that serves ads to this site. To find out more about how Engage Business Media manages the privacy of information in conjunction with serving ads on this site, please go to <http://www.engage.com/privacy/privacy.htm>.

Since Duke respects your right to privacy, when you register for our online services and email newsletters, you have the option to decline mailings about additional products and services from Duke or other parties to whom Duke may provide such information. When you subscribe to our print publications, you can elect to not receive third-party mailings by contacting our customer service using the contact information below. 

Duke uses reasonable precautions to keep the personal information that you disclose to us secure and to disclose such information only to third parties we believe to be responsible. However, we are not responsible for any breach of security or for any actions of any third parties which receive the information. Duke also links to many other sites and we are not responsible for their privacy policies or practices. 

At any time, you may correct your personal information or opt not to receive additional mailings by contacting Duke customer service at
