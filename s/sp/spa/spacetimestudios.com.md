**I. INFORMATION WE COLLECT**

**Connecting with the Spacetime Applications and Information Provided by You.**

We will collect personal information from you when you sign up for any Spacetime Applications, interact with our Spacetime Applications, including visiting our Sites, update your profile, interact with other users, and use various features of our Spacetime Applications. We may also collect information about you through other methods, including research surveys or information we obtain from third parties, including verification services and public sources. 

From time-to-time we may request information from you and other users via surveys, contests or sweepstakes. Participation in these surveys, contests or sweepstakes is completely voluntary so you and other users have a choice whether or not to disclose this information. The requested information typically includes contact information (such as name and address), and demographic information (such as zip code). Contact information may be used to notify the winners, award prizes and file winnerâ€™s information as required by law. **Information Collected Through Technology**

**Web Logs.** Like most standard website servers, we log IP addresses (a unique number assigned to every computer on the Internet) and device identification in order to improve security, analyze trends and administer the Spacetime Applications. We may track your movement within the Spacetime Applications, the pages from which you were referred, access times and browser types. This information may include the browser you are using, the Internet address from which you linked to our Spacetime Applications, the operating system of your computer, the unique IP address of the computer or device identification from the mobile device that you used to access our Spacetime Applications and usage and browsing habits. We use this information in the aggregate to gauge the effectiveness, usage, and popularity of our Spacetime Applications, track information for statistical purposes, help diagnose and troubleshoot server malfunctions, analyze trends, track user movement in the aggregate, and gather broad demographic information. We may also use individual information we collect through web logs to send you targeted offers and other communications.

**Cookies.** To help make sure you receive information that is relevant to you, our Spacetime Applications use data "cookies." We use session cookies to make it easier for you to navigate our Spacetime Applications. A session ID cookie expires when you close your browser. We use persistent cookies to differentiate visitors to our Spacetime Applications from registered users, to customize the content of certain sections of the Spacetime Applications and to enable us to track and cater to the interests of users, in order to enhance their experience on the Spacetime Applications. A persistent cookie remains on your hard drive for an extended period of time. You can remove persistent cookies by following directions provided in your Internet browser's "help" file. Cookies help us learn which areas of our Spacetime Applications are useful and which areas need improvement. We will not use cookies or other data capture mechanisms to collect any personally identifiable information about you. You may configure your browser to prevent cookies from being set on your computer. If you reject cookies, you may still use the Spacetime Applications, but your ability to use some areas of the Spacetime Applications may be limited, and you may not be able to use certain features of the Spacetime Applications, including playing certain Games or entering sweepstakes or contests. 

**Web Beacons.** Our web pages contain "web beacons." Web beacons allow us to count users that have visited a web page or opened an email that contains a web beacon and collect other types of aggregate information. Using this information, we are able to measure the effectiveness of our advertising and how visitors use our Spacetime Applications. This allows us to learn what pages are most attractive to our visitors, which parts of our Spacetime Applications are the most interesting and what kind of offers our registered users like to see. Some of the web beacons that are in place on our Spacetime Applications are used only by us, and other web beacons are from our vendors who use this data to assist us in analyzing how our Spacetime Applications are used.
