**Introduction**   
My Persona Limited respects each individual's right to personal privacy. We will collect and use information through our Web site and software only in the ways disclosed in this statement. 

**What Information Do We Collect?**   
My Persona collects information at several points. We collect this information through various methods including but not limited to registration forms, clickstream tracking and automatic logging files. My Persona does not collect any unique or personally identifiable information about you (for example, name and email address) except when you agree to the terms of the End User License agreement. 

My Persona does not actively market to children, and we never knowingly ask a child under 13 to divulge personal information. 

We may use third-party advertising companies to service ads on our behalf. These companies and My Persona may employ cookies and action tags (also known as single pixel gifs or web beacons) to measure advertising effectiveness on our site. A cookie is a small text file that our web server places on a user's computer hard drive to be a unique identifier. Cookies and action tags enable My Persona to track usage patterns, authenticate users and deliver customized content to users. 

**How do we use your information**   
The information collected by My Persona will be used solely for the purposes enabling you to use the My Persona service and to provide you information about the My Persona service. MyEmo will not share personally identifiable information with any third party without your express consent. We will not share data with any third party. 

Registered users will receive additional announcements from us about products, services, special deals, and a newsletter. Out of respect for the privacy of our users we present the option to not receive these types of communications in the future 

**Questions**   
If you have any further questions about privacy or security, please contact us by sending an email to: [info@myemoticons.com](mailto:info@myemoticons.com)
